TerraCurve Tower is an architectural design project that reimagines the traditional skyscraper through the lens of computational design and sustainable materials, by exploring interoperability between Rhino and Revit to optimize the design process.

about.

In the Rethinking BIM course. a mixed-use project was developed for the NOMAD neighborhood-a transitional area north of Madison Square Park and east of Penn Station-to address evolving urban needs. The project responded to a developer's brief to create a design that caters to workers. tourists, and local residents through a mix of creative office/light manufacturing. retail. and hotel components. while considering post-COVID patterns of work and travel.
The project employed Rhino and Grasshopper for massing studies. facade design, and various analysis. transitioning to Revit using Rhino.Inside for precise drawings and documentation

The design concept centers on creating a building that harmoniously bridges traditional and modern New York architectural aesthetics The structure is oriented to frame terraces with a clear view of the Empire State Building, providing a dynamic urban backdrop. A significant park is positioned above the lobby level and integrated with the retail floors, serving as a multifunctional space featuring food courts and recreational areas that cater to both workers and hotel guests.
The facade transitions from a robust terracotta treatment at the base-echoing the city's traditional exposed brick structures-to a thinner. glass-dominated upper section. symbolizing the evolution toward modern skyscraper design while maintaining contextual sensitivity to the surrounding historic brick buildings.
